PROJECT OVERVIEW

Located on the eastern edge of the Mumbai Metro Region and less than 15.1kms from the economically integral MIDC industrial estate of Thane, this is an important new district that abuts the suburban railway station of Datta Nagri. Designed as a sustainable community, the project boasts of a combined school, shopping centre, the ‘village green’, parks, children’s play areas and playing fields lending the overall development a real country feel. Additional amenities include a cinema and an unrivalled shopping experience of the mall. Pearl, Datta Nagri (Thane) is an ideal location for commuters with a direct service from CST station and Thane which is less than 30 minutes away.

LOCATION ADVANTAGES

Datta Nagri is a suburban railway station of Mumbai. All suburban trains on the Thane-Khopoli line stop here. It is less than 10 kms from the Mumbai manufacturing suburb, Thane, which is currently under a major overhaul. Datta Nagri sits on the periphery of the 12 lane multi-modal ring road that is planned to decongest the vehicular and cargo traffic of Mumbai city.
Fueled by a substantial infrastructural growth, Datta Nagri (Thane) is expected to be a leading residential destination across the Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR) which is a metropolitan area consisting of the Mumbai metropolis and its satellite towns.

About Us

We understand India’s need for smarter, efficient and sustainable cities which offer a higher quality of life to its people. With a goal and vision of building “100 future ready cities by 2030”, Pearl is embarking on a new journey that lays the benchmark for quality and modern urban development in the country. The brand is driven by technology in all they do from construction & operations, enabling customers to measure, manage and pay for services that they use. Pearl matches the global trend of increasing affordability by designing compact homes and offering standard amenities including free car parking, a community center with a swimming pool and commercial complexes. This year alone Pearl built cities across 18 locations in India and launched 40,000 houses spread across Maharashtra. At Pearl, the aim is not to build only houses, but cities that are environmentally friendly, technologically advanced, committed to education, dedicated to health, and that foster community spirit.
